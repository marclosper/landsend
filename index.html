REMOTE, EXTREME, CHALLENGING, PERFECT

Cape Agulhas, southernmost tip of the African continent. Here the Atlantic and Indian oceans meet in a wild alchemic wind-blown landscape where nature and viticulture combine to create extreme grape-farming conditions. Presiding over these two oceans is the famous lighthouse that has been in operation since 1849. Over the past three-and-a-half centuries, hundreds of ships have fallen victim to the treacherous conditions, succumbing to the tempests that have left their wrecks scattered along this South Cape coastline.

UNCHARTERED TERRITORY, UNLIMITED POTENTIAL

Some 40km from Agulhas lies Elim town, capital of the Cape South Coast wine region, South Africa's coolest wine growing region and home to some of the country’s most distinctive and expressive wines. This is hardy farmer country with only a handful of producers and scarcely 150ha planted to vines. These vineyards are the only ones in the world that can claim they are exposed to the maritime influence of two of the world’s seven oceans. As recently as the 1990’s, the land was farmed agriculturally and nobody had attempted planting vineyards. The first experimental plantings produced fruit of unique quality and the potential of this remote wine region began to spread to wine critics and wine lovers far and wide.

Courageous Winemaking, Not For The Faint-Hearted

Elim is largely Sauvignon Blanc country due to the cool growing conditions required to express the variety’s best assets. Syrah flourishes in this exposed part of the country making wines that express their sense of place.

tasting notes

This classic wine exudes a bouquet of wild tropical fruit, intermingled with hints of flint and freshly picked herbs. These flavours lead to a luscious mouth filling palate of ripe kiwi fruit and Cape gooseberry with a long mineral finish. Enjoy with crayfish, oysters or sautéed asparagus spears.
